Mountain Crest Lodge

Client

Fischer Family

COMPLETION

October 15, 2024

PROJECT TYPE

Private Retreat

DESIGNER

Emily Dawson

A sanctuary above the clouds, where modern design meets the untamed wilderness. Mountain Crest Lodge is more than just a mountain retreat—it is an immersive experience in nature. Designed to blend with the dramatic alpine landscape, this lodge combines rugged materials, expansive views, and sustainable design to create a timeless escape.

Architectural Harmony with the Terrain

Positioned on the crest of a secluded mountain range, the lodge was designed to integrate naturally with its surroundings. The structure follows the contours of the land, reducing excavation and preserving the integrity of the landscape. A combination of stone foundations and wooden beams ensures durability while giving the home a rustic yet refined character. The sloped roofline mimics the silhouette of the peaks beyond, reinforcing the lodge’s deep connection to the environment.

A Warm and Inviting Interior

Stepping inside, guests are greeted by a grand double-height living space centered around a monumental stone fireplace. The use of natural materials—aged oak, raw stone, and soft wool—creates a sense of warmth and comfort. Large windows frame uninterrupted mountain views, making nature the focal point of the design. Cozy nooks, built-in seating, and an open-plan kitchen and dining area encourage relaxation and togetherness, making the lodge an ideal retreat for families and guests.

Sustainable Living in a Remote Landscape

Given its remote location, self-sufficiency was a key priority in the design. The lodge utilizes a combination of solar and geothermal energy to maintain comfortable temperatures year-round. Thick insulation, triple-glazed windows, and thermal mass stone walls contribute to passive heating, reducing reliance on external energy sources. Rainwater harvesting and an advanced filtration system provide clean water, while responsibly sourced wood fuels the fireplaces, adding to the home’s eco-conscious design.

Outdoor Spaces That Connect with Nature

Beyond its interiors, the lodge offers a variety of outdoor living spaces designed for every season. A spacious wraparound terrace provides a perfect spot for morning coffee with panoramic sunrise views. An outdoor fire pit area offers a gathering space for stargazing, while a hidden spa-like hot tub allows for relaxation even in the depths of winter. Trails leading from the lodge connect directly to hiking paths, immersing residents in the surrounding alpine beauty.

A Refuge in the Wilderness

Mountain Crest Lodge is not just a home—it is an escape from the everyday, a place where the beauty of the mountains is always present. Whether covered in snow or bathed in golden autumn light, the lodge offers a timeless connection to nature, balancing luxury with the raw beauty of the wilderness.

Services Provided

Architecture

A contemporary alpine retreat designed to embrace the rugged beauty of the mountains while providing warmth and shelter from the elements. The structure balances modern aesthetics with traditional materials like timber and stone.

Interior

A cozy yet sophisticated design featuring exposed wooden beams, floor-to-ceiling fireplaces, and panoramic glass walls. The interior layout is carefully planned to maximize natural light and breathtaking mountain views.

Sustainability

Passive heating strategies, high-efficiency insulation, and renewable energy sources ensure that the lodge operates sustainably while blending seamlessly into its natural surroundings.
